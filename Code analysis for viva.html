DeepDive:How the Project Works

Pipeline StageWhat Happens Under the HoodWhy It Matters
1Minicorpus assemblyA handful of handpicked sentences for each author are stored in author_corpus.This is the only training data. We purposely keep it tiny to show how farpretrainedmodels and clever heuristics can go with very little data.
2Style fingerprintingSentenceTransformer('allmpnetbasev2') turns every sentence into a 768dimensional vector. We then average those vectors for each author one centroid per author.That centroid is an embedding of style: vocabulary, syntax and semantic patterns are all baked into its position in vector space.
3Heuristic text generatorInstead of a full languagemodel, generate_text() concatenates: 1)a stylespecific starter (Well now, for Twain, My dear sir, for Austen); 2)random connectors; 3)a tail; plus dummy tokens to reach the requested length.Its intentionally lightweight: no GPU inference, instant generation, easy to read code.
4Multimetric evaluationCosine similarity to each author centroid Who does this look like?
BLEU compares ngram overlap with the reference corpus.
ROUGE captures longer phrase overlap.
Textstat reports readability (Flesch, SMOG).		A single metric can be misleading; multiple metrics give a fuller picture: style proximity and linguistic quality.
5Result dictionaryFunction returns a tidy dict: {similarity, bleu, rouge, readability, predicted_author}.Easy to log, plot, or feed into dashboards.

Likely Viva Questions & Suggested Talking Points

  1. Why average embeddings instead of keeping all sentences?
    Averaging creates a compact prototype; cosine distance to the centroid is fast and memorylight.

  2. Would TFIDF vectors work?
    Yes, but transformer embeddings capture semantics and syntax, outperforming bagofwords on style similarity.

  3. Limitations of the current generator?
    It ignores deep syntax and coherence; future work (sectionA) upgrades to an LLM for realism.

  4. Difference between BLEU and ROUGEwhy both?
    BLEU favors precision (how many generated ngrams appear in reference). ROUGE favors recall (how much reference is covered). Together they balance each other.

  5. How do readability metrics influence generation?
    They provide a feedback loopgenerator parameters can be tuned until Flesch/SMOG hit a target band.

  6. Ethical concerns?
    Potential misuse for deepfake text; mitigation includes watermarks, transparency, and limiting highfidelity generation to authorized contexts.
